Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • Warranty

    • + What is the scope of my warranty?

      The Consumer Act of the Philippines (RA7394) provides consumers the right to seek Repair, Replacement, and Refund as relief in case a consumer product is found to be defective or has an imperfection. In case of Repair, work must conform to the express warranty within 30 days which may be extended by agreement, while Replacement may be elected when the product continues to have a defect or malfunction after a reasonable number of attempts to remedy the problem. If Replacement is not possible, a Refund is allowed but the amount attributable to the use of the consumer prior to the discovery of non-conformity shall be deducted. These remedies are available at no cost to you as the consumer.

      If the product is covered by an express warranty review the terms and conditions provided therein so you may be guided. Should there be no express warranty, the seller, distributor, and/or manufacturer is responsible for implied warranty against defects which shall endure for not less than sixty (60) days nor more than one year following the sale of new consumer products.

      Should there be no action on the part of the establishment and you wish to pursue a complaint, you may file the same by registering at and providing the required details on our consumer complaints website at https://consumercare.dti.gov.ph/#/home

    • + What are express and implied warranty?

      Express warranties are written promises by the seller to the quality of the product. These express warranties specify what the seller’s obligations are in case the product fails and how long a warranty is valid for. If no warranty card or certificate was handed to you upon purchase, then there is an implied warranty that protects your rights as a consumer to seek repair, replacement, and refund within sixty (60) days to one (1) year. In this case, the receipt acts as the warranty card.

    • + Secondhand Products

      Generally, there is no implied warranty in the sale of secondhand articles. Second-hand products do not carry any guarantee and cannot be returned or replaced if they turn out to be defective. The seller or vendor is only liable in the sale of a secondhand product if the seller provides an express written warranty to warrant the general fitness or quality of the product sold to the buyer. Thus, we advise that you revisit the terms and conditions of your contract and check if there is any express written warranty provided therein.

    • + Can we exchange a defective product that we purchased?

      According to the Consumer Act, consumers have the right to have the product repaired, replaced, or refunded if the purchased product is defective or damaged. Also, Repairs must be made within 30 days, but this can be extended if agreed upon.

    • + How do I know if it is still within the warranty period?

      We can check the warranty card. If there is no express warranty, in other words, no written warranty, no warranty card, there is an implied warranty against hidden defects. This will be no less than 60 days and no more than one year.

    • + What if the seller cannot replace the defective product?

      If the seller cannot replace the product, the seller must refund the consumer's payment but must deduct the amount equivalent to the consumer's use before discovering the defect.

    • + What if I change my mind and no longer want the product?

      The seller can refuse to replace a product or refund the amount paid if the request is by virtue of the consumer’s change of mind or mistake and not due to product imperfection or defect. However, some businesses implement an internal policy that allows refunds only as an act of goodwill to consumers.

  • No Return No Exchange Policy

    • + What is No Return No Exchange Policy?

      No Return, No Exchange is not enforced by the DTI. It is prohibited under the Consumer Act of the Philippines or RA 7394. According to the law, it is prohibited to put the words “No Return, No Exchange” on receipts, in any area of ​the store, or on any document proving the sale.

    • + Why is it prohibited to include a “No Return No Exchange” policy on receipts or any document to prove the sale?

      This is because it violates the consumer's right to have the product repaired, replaced, or refunded if the purchased product is or has been defective or damaged since purchase. If you remember, these are our 3Rs – repair, replace, refund.

    • + Can I ask for a refund if I changed my mind and do not want the product anymore?

      No. The seller can refuse to replace a product or refund the amount paid if the request is by virtue of the consumer’s change of mind or mistake and not due to product imperfection or defect. However, some businesses implement an internal policy that allows refunds only as an act of goodwill to consumers.

      Aside from a change in mind, please referee to the Warranty section for other situations.

  • Senior Citizen and Person with Disability Discount

    • + Who is a Senior Citizen?

      A Senior Citizen or elderly is any Filipino citizen who is a resident of the Philippines, and who is sixty (60) years old or above. It may apply to senior citizens with “dual citizenship” status, provided they prove their Filipino citizenship and have at least six (6) months residency in the Philippines. [RA9994]

    • + Who is a Person with Disability?

      A Person With Disability is a person who has a long-term physical, mental, intellectual or sensory impairment which in interaction with various barriers may hinder their full and effective participation in society on an equal basis with others. [IRR of RA 10754]

    • + What is the law governing Senior Citizen Discount and Person with Disability Discount?

      The law governing Senior Citizens discount is Republic Act No. 9994 (RA 9994), or the Expanded Senior Citizens Act of 2010, amending RA Nos.7432 and 9257, while the law governing the rights and privileged for Persons With Disability is RA 10754, or An Act Expanding the Benefits and Privileges of Persons with Disability, amending RA Nos. 7277, 9442 and 10070.

  • Price Tag

    • + What is Price Tag?

      According to the Consumer Act of the Philippines, a price tag is a label that states the price of a product to provide consumers with sufficient information and guidance to compare the prices of products.

    • + Where should the price tag be placed?

      According to DTI Department Administrative Order No. 09, series of 2002, the price tag must be properly placed on the shelf or rack where the product in question is displayed. It must be secured to minimize misplacement, tampering, or loss.

    • + What if the price on the shelf tag is different from the counter or cashier?

      The stores must ensure that the price on the shelf, on the tag, or on the checker is the same price at the counter or at the cashier.
